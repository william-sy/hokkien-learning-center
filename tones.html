🎵 Hokkien Tone Chart

Taiwanese Hokkien has 7 distinct tones (tone 6 is merged into tone 2 in most modern varieties). Pitch levels run from 1 (lowest) to 5 (highest) on each diagram — the line shows how the pitch moves over the duration of the syllable. Short vertical ticks mark checked tones (abrupt stop endings).

💡 Dialect note: These contours reflect standard Taiwanese Hokkien (臺灣閩南語). Malaysian and Singaporean varieties share the same 7-tone system but individual pitch values may vary slightly. Tone is the single most important aspect of Hokkien pronunciation — the same syllable in different tones means completely different things.

POJ Diacritic Quick Reference

Pe̍h-ōe-jī (POJ) marks tone with diacritics placed on the main vowel of each syllable. Tâi-lô uses the same system. For syllables with multiple vowels, the mark goes on the most prominent vowel following the rule: a/e/o > i/u > m/n.

2陰上 High Falling51 Acute ´á, é, í, ó, ú 虎 hó (tiger)
3陰去 Low Falling21 Grave `à, è, ì, ò, ù 意 ì (meaning)
4陰入 Short Lowˀ3 Stop final-p, -t, -k, -h (short) 一 it (one)
5陽平 Low Rising24 Circumflex ^â, ê, î, ô, û 人 lâng (person)
7陽去 Mid Level33 Macron ¯ā, ē, ī, ō, ū 字 jī (character)
8陽入 Short Highˀ5 Superscript dot · + stop finala̍, e̍, i̍, o̍, u̍ + -p/-t/-k/-h 學 o̍h (to study)
💡 Where to put the mark: If there is an a or e, mark it (e.g. àu). If there is an o, mark it (e.g. óng). Otherwise mark the last vowel. Nasal finals m and ng can also carry the mark when they are the nucleus.

Tone Sandhi (連讀變調)

In connected speech, a non-final syllable changes to its sandhi tone. Only the last syllable of a tone group keeps its citation (dictionary) tone. This is one of the most important — and most distinctive — features of Hokkien.

Citation tone→Sandhi toneExample
Tone 1 (44 High Level)→Tone 7 (33 Mid Level)好 hó (T2) + 吃 tsia̍h → hō-tsia̍h "eat well"
Tone 2 (51 High Falling)→Tone 1 (44 High Level)虎 hó → ho before another syllable
Tone 3 (21 Low Falling)→Tone 2 (51 High Falling)意 ì → í before another syllable
Tone 4 (ˀ3 Short Low)→Tone 8 (ˀ5 Short High)一 it → i̍t before another syllable
Tone 5 (24 Low Rising)→Tone 7 (33 Mid Level)人 lâng → lāng before another syllable
Tone 7 (33 Mid Level)→Tone 5 (24 Low Rising)字 jī → jî before another syllable
Tone 8 (ˀ5 Short High)→Tone 4 (ˀ3 Short Low)學 o̍h → oh before another syllable
💡 Tones 1, 7 and 2, 1 form cycles: T1→T7 and T7→T5 are the most noticeable changes in everyday speech. Memorising a few common compound words is the fastest way to internalise sandhi naturally.
